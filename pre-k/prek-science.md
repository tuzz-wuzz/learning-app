# Pre-Kindergarten Science Curriculum

Aligned with Head Start Early Learning Outcomes Framework (ELOF) for Scientific Inquiry.

---

## Core Practices
- Observing with the five senses
- Asking "What happens if...?"
- Making simple predictions
- Describing what is seen, heard, felt, and smelled
- Using simple tools (magnifying glasses, scoops)

---

## Unit 1: My Body & My Senses (Weeks 1–6)

### Big Idea
I use my body to learn about my world.

### Learning Goals
- Name the five senses (sight, hearing, touch, smell, taste)
- Identify body parts and their functions
- Use senses to explore different materials

### Week-by-Week Breakdown

| Week | Topic | Key Activities |
|------|-------|----------------|
| 1 | Exploring Sight | Using magnifying glasses, "I Spy" colors |
| 2 | Exploring Sound | Listening jars, sound matching, quiet vs. loud |
| 3 | Exploring Touch | Texture boards (fuzzy, rough, sticky), mystery bag |
| 4 | Exploring Smell | Scented playdough, flower smelling, kitchen smells |
| 5 | Exploring Taste | Sweet vs. sour (safety first!), snack exploration |
| 6 | My Whole Body | "Head, Shoulders, Knees, and Toes," body tracings |

---

## Unit 2: The World Outside (Weather & Seasons) (Weeks 7–12)

### Big Idea
The world outside changes and I can observe those changes.

### Learning Goals
- Identify basic weather types (sunny, rainy, snowy)
- Dress appropriately for the weather
- Observe changes in trees and sky

### Week-by-Week Breakdown

| Week | Topic | Key Activities |
|------|-------|----------------|
| 7 | Sunny & Rainy | Water table "rain," making sun masks |
| 8 | Windy & Snowy | Blowing bubbles, paper snowflake cutting |
| 9 | Fall Changes | Leaf collecting, sorting by color, crunching leaves |
| 10 | Winter Cold | Feeling ice, dressing a "weather bear" |
| 11 | Spring Growth | Finding puddles, looking for buds on trees |
| 12 | Summer Heat | Shadow play, staying cool with water |

---

## Unit 3: Living Things (Plants & Animals) (Weeks 13–20)

### Big Idea
Plants and animals are alive and need special things to grow.

### Learning Goals
- Distinguish between living and non-living things
- Understand that plants need water and light
- Identify common animals and their sounds
- Know that babies grow into adults

### Week-by-Week Breakdown

| Week | Topic | Key Activities |
|------|-------|----------------|
| 13 | Alive or Not? | Sorting toys (not alive) vs. classroom plants (alive) |
| 14 | Seeds to Plants | Planting grass seeds in a cup, watering |
| 15 | What Plants Need | "Feeding" the plant water and sun |
| 16 | Animal Sounds | Matching animal figures to their sounds |
| 17 | Animal Movement | Jumping like frogs, crawling like caterpillars |
| 18 | Farm Animals | Sorting farm vs. zoo animals |
| 19 | Pets | Caring for a pretend pet, fish tank observation |
| 20 | Human Growth | Looking at baby pictures vs. now |

---

## Unit 4: Exploring Materials (Physics) (Weeks 21–28)

### Big Idea
I can change how things move and how they feel.

### Learning Goals
- Investigate "Sink or Float"
- Explore magnets (attract vs. not)
- Use ramps to move toy cars
- Mix colors to see what happens

### Week-by-Week Breakdown

| Week | Topic | Key Activities |
|------|-------|----------------|
| 21 | Sink or Float | Predicting and testing objects in the water table |
| 22 | Water Power | Using funnels, wheels, and scoops |
| 23 | Magnets | Magnet wands and a "treasure hunt" for metal |
| 24 | Ramps & Slides | Rolling balls down tubes and ramps |
| 25 | Push & Pull | Moving heavy boxes vs. light feathers |
| 26 | Color Mixing | Red + Blue = Purple (using water and food coloring) |
| 27 | Bubbles | Exploring different shapes of bubble wands |
| 28 | Building & Toppling | Exploring balance with different block types |

---

## Unit 5: Our Earth (Weeks 29–36)

### Big Idea
The Earth is full of interesting things like dirt, rocks, and water.

### Learning Goals
- Explore different types of rocks and soil
- Understand that we should take care of our school and world
- Identify day and night

### Week-by-Week Breakdown

| Week | Topic | Key Activities |
|------|-------|----------------|
| 29 | Rocks | Collecting and washing rocks, sorting by size |
| 30 | Dirt & Mud | Digging in the sensory bin, "mud" painting |
| 31 | Sand | Sifting, pouring, and wet vs. dry sand |
| 32 | Water | Pouring, splashing, and ice melting |
| 33 | Day Sky | Looking for the sun and clouds |
| 34 | Night Sky | Making star constellations with stickers |
| 35 | Trash & Recycling | Sorting "paper" vs "plastic" in the classroom |
| 36 | Earth Celebration | Planting a class tree or flower, nature walk |

---

## Materials
- Magnifying glasses and child-safe tweezers
- Water table and sensory bins
- Magnets (large wands)
- Ramps and gutters
- Flashlights for shadow play
- Planting cups, soil, and seeds
- Prism or sun catchers
- Sortable rock and shell collections
