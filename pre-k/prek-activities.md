# Pre-Kindergarten Learning Activities & Games

This guide provides sensory, gross motor, and play-based activities for Pre-K students. These focus on developmental milestones and early social skills.

---

## Activity 1: Sensory Discovery Bin
**Subjects:** Science (Senses), Math (Sorting), Fine Motor

### Description
Fill a large bin with a base material (rice, beans, sand, water, or shredded paper) and hide small objects inside. Students use scoops, tweezers, or their hands to find and sort the "treasures."

### Differentiation
- **Make it Easier:** Use large objects that are easy to see and grab. Use a very shallow layer of material.
- **Make it Harder:** Hide small letters or numbers. Ask students to find all the "red" items or all the "circle" items.

### Inclusive Strategies
- **For Sensory Processing Disorder:** Provide gloves or tools (shovels/scoops) so students don't have to touch the material directly.
- **For Visual Impairment:** Use high-contrast objects and materials with distinct textures (e.g., smooth pebbles in rough sand).

## Activity 2: Animal Movement Parade
**Subjects:** Science (Animals), PE (Gross Motor), Music

### Description
Put on some music and call out different animal names. Students move around the room like that animal until the music stops (Freeze!).

### Activities
- "Hop like a frog!"
- "Slither like a snake!"
- "Stomp like an elephant!"
- "Fly like a bird!"

### Differentiation
- **Make it Easier:** Show a large picture of the animal while calling it out. Focus on only two animals at a time.
- **Make it Harder:** Ask students to make the animal's sound while moving. Change the music speed (fast for cheetah, slow for turtle).

### Inclusive Strategies
- **For Physical Disabilities:** Encourage students to move their arms or head like the animal, or use a "magic wand" to represent the animal's movement from their seat.
- **For ADHD:** Use a "Ready, Set, Go" signal and a clear "Stop" (Freeze) to practice impulse control.

## Activity 3: Bubble Wrap Stomp & Pop
**Subjects:** Science (Force), PE (Coordination), Sensory

### Description
Tape large sheets of bubble wrap to the floor. Students walk, jump, or crawl across to hear the "pops."

### Differentiation
- **Make it Easier:** Tape small pieces to a table for finger-popping.
- **Make it Harder:** Place colored dots on the bubbles and ask them to only pop the "blue" ones.

### Inclusive Strategies
- **For Hearing Impairments:** This is highly tactile. Encourage students to "feel" the vibrations of the pops through their feet or hands.
- **For Dyspraxia:** This provides great feedback for body awareness. Use a hand for balance if needed.

## Activity 4: Collaborative Block City
**Subjects:** Social-Emotional (Sharing), Math (Geometry), History (Community)

### Description
Provide a large set of blocks and a designated "construction zone." Students work together to build a city with roads, houses, and schools.

### Differentiation
- **Make it Easier:** Provide large, soft foam blocks. Focus on just "building a tall tower" together.
- **Make it Harder:** Add "Community Helper" figures and ask them where the "doctor" or "firefighter" should live.

### Inclusive Strategies
- **For ASD:** Provide "Building Cards" with pictures of what they can build to reduce the open-ended nature of the task. Assign clear roles (e.g., "The Loader" and "The Builder").
- **For Language Delays:** Model words like "up," "on," "next to," and "my turn" during the play.

---

## Activity 5: Water Table "Wash Station"
**Subjects:** Science (Properties of Water), Life Skills, Sensory

### Description
Set up the water table with bubbles and sponges. Provide toy cars, dolls, or plastic animals that need "washing."

### Differentiation
- **Make it Easier:** Use plain water without bubbles if the texture is too much. Provide large sponges.
- **Make it Harder:** Add "mud" (brown paint or dirt) to the toys first so they have to scrub them clean.

### Inclusive Strategies
- **For Sensory Avoidance:** Use clear water and a single brush or sponge to keep hands relatively dry.
- **For Fine Motor Struggles:** Use large, easy-to-grip scrubbing brushes instead of small sponges.

## General Strategies for Pre-K Support

### For Early Literacy/Dyslexia Readiness
- **Multisensory Letters:** Trace letters in sand, salt, or shaving cream.
- **Rhyme & Chant:** Use repetitive songs to build phonological awareness.

### For Fine Motor/Dysgraphia Readiness
- **Vertical Surfaces:** Tape paper to the wall to draw. This builds shoulder and wrist strength.
- **Playdough:** Squeezing and rolling strengthens the "pencil grip" muscles.

### For Math/Dyscalculia Readiness
- **Real Objects:** Always count real things (snack pieces, buttons) rather than just pictures.
- **Subitizing:** Play games with large foam dice to recognize small quantities (1, 2, 3) without counting.
