# Pre-Kindergarten Computer & Digital Activities

This guide focuses on early digital literacy, cause-and-effect, and intentional interaction with technology for Pre-K students.

---

## Guidelines for Pre-K Technology
- **Time Limits:** 10–15 minutes per session.
- **Adult Support:** Active co-viewing or co-playing is recommended.
- **Physical Tools:** Touchscreens are preferred over mice for 3-4 year olds.

---

## Activity 1: Cause & Effect "Magic"
**Subjects:** Logic, Fine Motor, Science

### Description
Using simple "cause and effect" apps (like Sago Mini World, Toca Boca, or Peek-a-Boo apps), students touch the screen to see an immediate reaction (e.g., a character jumps, a flower grows).

### Differentiation
- **Make it Easier:** Use apps where *any* touch results in a reaction.
- **Make it Harder:** Use apps that require a "drag and drop" movement to trigger the effect.

### Inclusive Strategies
- **For Severe Motor Impairments:** Use a large external "switch" button that triggers the action on the screen.
- **For ASD:** Use apps with "quiet" modes to avoid sudden or loud startling noises.

---

## Activity 2: Digital Finger Painting
**Subjects:** Art, Fine Motor

### Description
Using a simple drawing app (like Seesaw Drawing, Doodle Buddy, or Tayasui Sketches School), students use their finger to "paint" with different colors and brush sizes.

### Differentiation
- **Make it Easier:** Provide a single color and focus on just making any mark.
- **Make it Harder:** Ask the student to draw a "circle" or their favorite animal.

### Inclusive Strategies
- **For Dysgraphia Readiness:** This is a zero-pressure way to practice the arm/hand movements used in writing.
- **For Visual Impairments:** Use apps with high-contrast colors and "thick" brush settings.

---

## Activity 3: Storybook "Listening"
**Subjects:** English (Comprehension), Listening Skills

### Description
Students use a digital storybook app (like Epic!, Vooks, or TumbleBooks) that highlights words as they are read aloud by a narrator.

### Differentiation
- **Make it Easier:** Use "picture only" stories where the student clicks to hear the name of the object.
- **Make it Harder:** Ask the student to retell one thing that happened in the story after they finish.

### Inclusive Strategies
- **For Dyslexia Readiness:** The "word highlighting" feature helps build the connection between spoken and written language.
- **For Hearing Impairments:** Use books with clear, slow animations that mirror the story's action.

---

## Activity 4: Simple Math Sorting (Digital)
**Subjects:** Math (Sorting, Counting)

### Description
Using an interactive game (like Khan Academy Kids or ABCya Pre-K), students sort digital items by color or shape.

### Differentiation
- **Make it Easier:** Sorting only 2 types of items (e.g., red apples and green apples).
- **Make it Harder:** Counting to 5 while dragging items into a digital basket.

### Inclusive Strategies
- **For Dyscalculia Readiness:** Use apps that provide immediate auditory feedback (e.g., the computer says "One, Two, Three" as the items are moved).
- **For ADHD:** Ensure the tablet is in "Guided Access" mode so the student cannot leave the learning game.

---

## Recommended Tools for Pre-K

### Apps & Websites
- **Khan Academy Kids:** Entirely free, high-quality curriculum with no ads.
- **Sago Mini / Toca Boca:** Excellent for creative, open-ended "digital dollhouse" play.
- **Starfall (Pre-K section):** Great for letter sounds and counting.
- **Vooks:** Animated storybooks that are paced perfectly for young children.

### Accessibility & Safety
- **Guided Access:** Essential for keeping students focused and safe.
- **Volume Limiters:** Use kid-safe headphones to protect hearing.
- **Stylus Pens:** Large, "crayon-style" styluses can help students practice their grip in a digital format.

---

## Tips for the Teacher
1. **Model Digital Citizenship:** Talk about "gentle hands" with technology and "asking before using."
2. **Balance is Key:** Technology should supplement, not replace, physical blocks, sand, and books.
3. **Screen Brightness:** Keep brightness at a comfortable level for young eyes.
4. **Cleanliness:** Use alcohol-free wipes to sanitize screens and headphones daily.
