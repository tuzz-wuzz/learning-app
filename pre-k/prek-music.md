# Pre-Kindergarten Music Curriculum

Aligned with Head Start Early Learning Outcomes Framework (ELOF) for Creative Arts Expression.

---

## Program Goals
- Participate in group singing and music-making
- Explore various sound-making objects and instruments
- Move creatively to different types of music
- Develop a sense of steady beat through movement and instruments
- Discriminate between sounds (high/low, fast/slow, loud/soft)

---

## Unit 1: My Voice & Sound (Weeks 1–6)

### Learning Goals
- Identify different ways to use the voice (speaking, singing, whispering, calling)
- Experiment with making high and low sounds
- Imitate environmental sounds with the voice

### Week-by-Week Breakdown

| Week | Topic | Key Activities |
|------|-------|----------------|
| 1 | Finding My Voice | Name chants, whispering vs. calling games |
| 2 | High & Low | Moving like a bird (high) or a bear (low) |
| 3 | Animal Sounds | "Old MacDonald" with silly voice changes |
| 4 | Soft & Loud | "Open, Shut Them" with dynamic changes |
| 5 | Humming & Buzzing | Making "bee" sounds, humming favorite tunes |
| 6 | Song Favorites | Group choice: "Baby Shark" or "Wheels on the Bus" |

---

## Unit 2: Move to the Beat (Weeks 7–12)

### Learning Goals
- Move body in response to music
- Attempt to maintain a steady beat through clapping or marching
- Stop movement when music stops (freeze)

### Week-by-Week Breakdown

| Week | Topic | Key Activities |
|------|-------|----------------|
| 7 | Clapping the Beat | Simple clapping songs, heartbeat listening |
| 8 | Marching | Walking to a drum beat, "Sousa" marches |
| 9 | Fast & Slow | "The Goldilocks" tempo game |
| 10 | Freeze Dance | Responding to music starting and stopping |
| 11 | Ribbon Dancing | Moving colorful ribbons to flowing music |
| 12 | Scarf Play | "Popcorn Kernels" song with scarves |

---

## Unit 3: Rhythm & Instruments (Weeks 13–20)

### Learning Goals
- Explore various rhythm instruments (shakers, drums, bells)
- Play instruments gently and with care
- Follow "Start" and "Stop" signals while playing

### Week-by-Week Breakdown

| Week | Topic | Key Activities |
|------|-------|----------------|
| 13 | Egg Shakers | Shaking high, low, and all around |
| 14 | Rhythm Sticks | Tapping together, tapping the floor |
| 15 | Drum Play | "Boom, Boom, Boom" — loud vs. soft drumming |
| 16 | Jingle Bells | Making "snow" music, shaking to the beat |
| 17 | Triangle & Woodblock | Listening for "long" vs. "short" sounds |
| 18 | Instrument Parade | Marching around the room with instruments |
| 19 | Making Music Together | Following a "conductor" (teacher signals) |
| 20 | Homemade Shakers | Making shakers with cups and beans |

---

## Unit 4: Singing Games & Fingerplays (Weeks 21–28)

### Learning Goals
- Learn and repeat simple songs and rhymes
- Coordinate hand movements with lyrics (fingerplays)
- Participate in circle games

### Week-by-Week Breakdown

| Week | Topic | Key Activities |
|------|-------|----------------|
| 21 | Itsy Bitsy Spider | Fine motor fingerplay coordination |
| 22 | Five Little Ducks | Counting and singing together |
| 23 | Ring Around the Rosie | Group movement and "falling down" |
| 24 | If You're Happy... | Connecting emotions to music and movement |
| 25 | Head, Shoulders... | Vocabulary and body awareness through song |
| 26 | Hokey Pokey | Learning left and right (simplified) |
| 27 | London Bridge | Cooperative play and singing |
| 28 | Farmer in the Dell | Role-playing within a circle song |

---

## Unit 5: Listening & Imagining (Weeks 29–36)

### Learning Goals
- Listen quietly to short pieces of music
- Describe how music makes them feel (happy, sleepy, excited)
- Use music as a prompt for pretend play

### Week-by-Week Breakdown

| Week | Topic | Key Activities |
|------|-------|----------------|
| 29 | Sleepy Time Music | Lullabies and relaxing with blankets |
| 30 | Happy Music | "Zip-a-Dee-Doo-Dah" and dancing |
| 31 | Nature Sounds | Listening to rain and bird recordings |
| 32 | Carnival of the Animals | Moving like lions, swans, and elephants |
| 33 | Drawing to Music | Using crayons while listening to Vivaldi |
| 34 | Story Music | Listening to music that "tells a story" |
| 35 | Music "Show" | Performing a favorite song for another class |
| 36 | Year-End Celebration | Favorite songs and dances party |

## Core Song Repertoire
- Twinkle Twinkle Little Star
- The Alphabet Song
- B-I-N-G-O
- You Are My Sunshine
- Skinnamarink
- This Old Man
- Down by the Bay

---

## Materials
- Egg shakers and maracas
- Rhythm sticks and woodblocks
- Hand drums and bongo drums
- Jingle bells and triangles
- Scarves, ribbons, and beanbags
- Parachute for group games
- Music player with a variety of genres
- Simple "conductor" baton (or stick)
