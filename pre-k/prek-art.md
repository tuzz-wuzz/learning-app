# Pre-Kindergarten Art Curriculum

Focus: Process Art and Sensory Exploration.

---

## Program Goals
- Explore various art materials freely
- Develop fine motor control (squeezing, tearing, holding)
- Discover color and texture through hands-on play
- Express ideas and feelings through "mark-making"
- Build confidence in creative choices

---

## Unit 1: Scribbles & Marks (Weeks 1–6)

### Learning Goals
- Use various tools to make marks (fat crayons, markers, chalk)
- Understand that "I can make a mark"
- Explore movement of the arm and hand while drawing

### Week-by-Week Breakdown

| Week | Topic | Key Activities |
|------|-------|----------------|
| 1 | Free Scribbling | Large paper on the floor, fat crayons |
| 2 | Vertical & Horizontal | Drawing "up and down" and "side to side" |
| 3 | Circles & Loops | Making "round and round" marks in sand/shaving cream |
| 4 | Chalk Play | Drawing on the sidewalk, using wet vs. dry chalk |
| 5 | Dot & Line | Making "poking" marks and "sliding" marks |
| 6 | Drawing to Music | Fast music (fast scribbles) vs. slow music |

---

## Unit 2: Exploring Color (Weeks 7–12)

### Learning Goals
- Identify basic colors (Red, Blue, Yellow)
- Experience "magic" color mixing
- Use color to fill a space

### Week-by-Week Breakdown

| Week | Topic | Key Activities |
|------|-------|----------------|
| 7 | Seeing Red | Red collage (paper, fabric, buttons) |
| 8 | Sunny Yellow | Finger painting with yellow |
| 9 | Beautiful Blue | Painting with blue on blue paper |
| 10 | Color Mixing: Orange | Mixing red and yellow on paper |
| 11 | Color Mixing: Green | Mixing blue and yellow in a baggie |
| 12 | Rainbow Play | Sorting colorful items into a class rainbow |

---

## Unit 3: Sticky & Squishy (Weeks 13–18)

### Learning Goals
- Explore textures (tactile art)
- Use glue and stickers independently
- Manipulate soft materials

### Week-by-Week Breakdown

| Week | Topic | Key Activities |
|------|-------|----------------|
| 13 | Sticker Art | Free sticking on paper or cardboard |
| 14 | Glue Spots | Squeezing "dots" of glue and adding sequins |
| 15 | Paper Tearing | Tearing tissue paper and gluing for stained glass |
| 16 | Playdough Fun | Squeezing, rolling, and poking playdough |
| 17 | Sticky Paper | Using contact paper to make sun catchers |
| 18 | Texture Collage | Gluing "soft" (cotton) and "rough" (sandpaper) |

---

## Unit 4: Painting with Everything (Weeks 19–24)

### Learning Goals
- Use different tools to apply paint
- Experience the sensation of paint
- Understand basic painting routines (smocks on!)

### Week-by-Week Breakdown

| Week | Topic | Key Activities |
|------|-------|----------------|
| 19 | Finger Painting | Squishing paint with whole hands |
| 20 | Sponge Dabbing | Making "bumpy" marks with sponges |
| 21 | Car Painting | Rolling toy cars through paint |
| 22 | Nature Painting | Using leaves or sticks as brushes |
| 23 | Bubble Wrap Print | Painting on bubble wrap and pressing paper |
| 24 | Big Brush Art | Using large house-painting brushes with water |

---

## Unit 5: Building Things (Weeks 25–30)

### Learning Goals
- Understand 3D vs. 2D (flat vs. standing up)
- Use blocks and boxes to create
- Explore "form"

### Week-by-Week Breakdown

| Week | Topic | Key Activities |
|------|-------|----------------|
| 25 | Block Towers | Building up and knocking down |
| 26 | Box Sculptures | Taping small boxes together |
| 27 | Paper Chains | Linking paper strips (with help) |
| 28 | Playdough Cities | Using sticks and playdough to build |
| 29 | Collaborative Box | Decorating a giant appliance box together |
| 30 | Found Object Art | Gluing bottle caps and corks to cardboard |

---

## Unit 6: Seasonal Art (Weeks 31–36)

### Learning Goals
- Connect art to the world around us
- Celebrate personal growth in art

### Week-by-Week Breakdown

| Week | Topic | Key Activities |
|------|-------|----------------|
| 31 | Spring Flowers | Coffee filter flowers with markers/water |
| 32 | Leaf Prints | Using paint and real leaves |
| 33 | Sunshine Art | Paper plate suns with yellow strips |
| 34 | Rainy Day Art | Drip painting with blue watercolors |
| 35 | Self-Portrait | Drawing a face again (compare to Week 1) |
| 36 | Art Celebration | Gallery walk of the "best" process pieces |

---

## Materials
- Finger paint and tempera paint (washable!)
- Large, chunky crayons and markers
- Playdough and clay tools
- Contact paper and tissue paper
- Recycled boxes, tubes, and caps
- Glue sticks and liquid glue (bottles with tips)
- Smocks or old shirts
- Large rolls of paper
- Sensory materials (shaving cream, sand, salt)
