# Pre-Kindergarten English Language Arts Curriculum

Aligned with Head Start Early Learning Outcomes Framework (ELOF) for Literacy.

---

## Strand 1: Foundational Skills & Print Awareness (Weeks 1–12)

### Learning Goals
- Show interest in books and shared reading experiences
- Recognize own name in print
- Understand that print carries meaning
- Identify some letters, particularly those in own name
- Handle books correctly (front to back, turning pages)

### Week-by-Week Breakdown

| Week | Topic | Key Activities |
|------|-------|----------------|
| 1 | Welcome to school | Name tags, "cubby" labels, school tour |
| 2 | Parts of a book | Front cover, back cover, "reading" pictures |
| 3 | My name | Name puzzles, sensory name writing (sand/shaving cream) |
| 4 | Letters in my name | Letter hunts, name building with blocks |
| 5 | Environmental print | Recognizing signs (Stop, Target, Cheerios) |
| 6 | Letter shapes | Straight lines vs. curvy lines, pipe cleaner letters |
| 7 | Alphabet songs | Traditional and new alphabet chants |
| 8 | Upper- vs. lowercase | Sorting big and little letters |
| 9 | Writing marks | From scribbling to "mock letters" |
| 10 | Directionality | Left-to-right finger tracking |
| 11 | Story prediction | "What happens next?" with picture clues |
| 12 | Letter review | Alphabet bingo, letter matching |

### Standards Covered
- IT-LC 7 – Shows interest in and awareness of writing and print
- P-LIT 1 – Demonstrates awareness that symbols and print have meaning

## Strand 2: Phonological Awareness (Weeks 13–24)

### Learning Goals
- Recognize and produce rhyming words
- Clap out syllables in names and simple words
- Identify beginning sounds in familiar words
- Listen for and repeat rhythmic patterns in language

### Week-by-Week Breakdown

| Week | Topic | Key Activities |
|------|-------|----------------|
| 13 | Rhyming fun | Nursery rhymes, rhyming matching games |
| 14 | Silly rhymes | Creating "nonsense" rhymes |
| 15 | Clapping syllables | Clapping names (e.g., Char-lotte), body percussion |
| 16 | Word counting | Jumping for each word in a short sentence |
| 17 | Beginning sounds (m, s, a) | Sound bags, "I Spy" beginning sounds |
| 18 | Beginning sounds (t, p, c) | Picture sorting by sound |
| 19 | Alliteration | Silly sentences (e.g., "Silly Sam sings") |
| 20 | Ending sounds intro | Listening for the "stop" sound |
| 21 | Sound blending | Guessing the word from split sounds (e.g., c-at) |
| 22 | Compound words | "Butter" + "fly" = "Butterfly" puzzles |
| 23 | Rhythm & beat | Chanting to a steady beat |
| 24 | Phonological review | Rhyme time celebration, syllable parade |

### Standards Covered
- P-LIT 2 – Demonstrates emerging phonological awareness

## Strand 3: Oral Language & Vocabulary (Weeks 1–36, ongoing)

### Learning Goals
- Follow simple one- and two-step directions
- Use increasingly complex sentences to express needs and ideas
- Expand vocabulary through themed units and read-alouds
- Participate in conversations with peers and adults

### Recommended Read-Aloud Themes

| Quarter | Themes | Example Titles |
|---------|--------|----------------|
| Q1 | All about me & my friends | *Llama Llama Misses Mama*, *Friends* by Helme Heine |
| Q2 | Animals & nature | *Dear Zoo*, *The Very Hungry Caterpillar* |
| Q3 | Community & imagination | *The Little Engine That Could*, *Where the Wild Things Are* |
| Q4 | Transitions & growth | *Mouse’s First Day of School*, *The Carrot Seed* |

### Vocabulary Strategies
- "Word of the Day" with physical actions
- Descriptive talk during play ("I see you are using the *heavy* blue block")
- Visual vocabulary cards with real photos
- Encouraging "Why" and "How" questions

### Standards Covered
- P-LC 1 – Understands and responds to increasingly complex communication
- P-LC 4 – Expresses self in increasingly long and detailed sentences

---

## Strand 4: Emergent Writing (Weeks 1–36, ongoing)

### Learning Goals
- Use a variety of writing tools (crayons, markers, chalk)
- Understand that writing represents spoken words
- Attempt to write own name
- Draw pictures to tell a story or share information

### Writing Stages

| Stage | Focus | Activities |
|-------|-------|------------|
| Scribbling | 1–9 | Free exploration with various media |
| Letter-like forms | 10–18 | Creating shapes that look like letters |
| Strings of letters | 19–27 | Writing long lines of familiar letters |
| Invented spelling | 28–36 | Labeling pictures with beginning letters |

### Standards Covered
- P-LIT 6 – Writes for a variety of purposes using symbols and letters

---

## Materials
- Tactile letters (sandpaper, felt, magnetic)
- Big books for shared reading
- Name cards for all students
- Writing center with various paper and tools
- Puppets for oral language practice
- Audiobooks and listening station
