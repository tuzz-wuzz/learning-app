# Kindergarten English Language Arts Curriculum

Aligned with Common Core State Standards for English Language Arts (CCSS-ELA).

---

## Strand 1: Print Concepts & Phonological Awareness (Weeks 1–12)

### Learning Goals
- Recognize that spoken words are represented in written language
- Understand basic features of print (left-to-right, top-to-bottom, page-by-page)
- Recognize and name all upper- and lowercase letters
- Rhyme, blend, and segment syllables and sounds

### Week-by-Week Breakdown

| Week | Topic | Key Activities |
|------|-------|----------------|
| 1 | Book handling & print concepts | Parts of a book, how to hold a book, read-alouds |
| 2 | Letters Aa–Dd | Letter songs, tracing, letter hunts |
| 3 | Letters Ee–Hh | Alphabet books, letter sorting, sensory writing |
| 4 | Letters Ii–Ll | Letter matching, magnetic letters, name writing |
| 5 | Letters Mm–Pp | Letter stamps, playdough letters, alphabet puzzles |
| 6 | Letters Qq–Tt | Letter of the day, alphabet art |
| 7 | Letters Uu–Xx | Letter bingo, environmental print walk |
| 8 | Letters Yy–Zz & review | Alphabet sequencing, letter assessment |
| 9 | Rhyming words | Rhyming books, rhyming match games, songs |
| 10 | Syllables | Clapping syllables, syllable sorting, names |
| 11 | Beginning sounds | Sound sorting, picture cards, "I Spy" sounds |
| 12 | Ending sounds | Listening games, picture matching, word chains |

### Standards Covered
- RF.K.1 – Demonstrate understanding of the organization and basic features of print
- RF.K.2 – Demonstrate understanding of spoken words, syllables, and sounds (phonemes)

## Strand 2: Phonics & Word Recognition (Weeks 13–24)

### Learning Goals
- Know letter-sound correspondences for all 26 letters
- Read common high-frequency words by sight (the, of, to, you, she, my, is, his, her, do, does, a, I, we, and, are, they, have, was)
- Distinguish between similarly spelled words (e.g., cat vs. can)
- Decode simple CVC (consonant-vowel-consonant) words

### Week-by-Week Breakdown

| Week | Topic | Key Activities |
|------|-------|----------------|
| 13 | Short a words | Word building with letter tiles, CVC reading |
| 14 | Short i words | Decodable readers, word family sorts |
| 15 | Short o words | Magnetic letter word building, sound boxes |
| 16 | Short e words | Decodable readers, word ladders |
| 17 | Short u words | Word family charts, reading simple sentences |
| 18 | Review short vowels | Word sorts, dictation, fluency practice |
| 19 | Sight words set 1 | Word wall, flash cards, sight word games (the, a, I, is, it, in, to) |
| 20 | Sight words set 2 | Sentence building, word hunts (and, can, we, my, you, do, are) |
| 21 | Sight words set 3 | Reading in context, writing sentences (he, she, was, they, have, like, said) |
| 22 | Consonant blends (bl, cl, fl) | Blend reading, picture-word matching |
| 23 | Consonant blends (br, cr, dr, gr) | Blend books, blend sorting |
| 24 | Review & assessment | Running records, sight word checks, decodable books |

### Standards Covered
- RF.K.3 – Know and apply grade-level phonics and word analysis skills
- RF.K.3a – Demonstrate basic knowledge of one-to-one letter-sound correspondences
- RF.K.3c – Read common high-frequency words by sight
- RF.K.3d – Distinguish between similarly spelled words

## Strand 3: Reading Comprehension & Literature (Weeks 1–36, ongoing)

### Learning Goals
- Retell familiar stories with key details
- Identify characters, settings, and major events
- Ask and answer questions about key details in a text
- Compare and contrast adventures and experiences of characters
- Engage with a variety of genres: fiction, nonfiction, poetry, fairy tales

### Recommended Read-Aloud Schedule

| Quarter | Themes | Example Titles |
|---------|--------|----------------|
| Q1 | Family, friends, school | *The Kissing Hand*, *Chrysanthemum*, *David Goes to School* |
| Q2 | Animals, seasons, community | *The Snowy Day*, *Brown Bear Brown Bear*, *Corduroy* |
| Q3 | Fairy tales, folktales | *The Three Little Pigs*, *Goldilocks*, *The Ugly Duckling* |
| Q4 | Growth, change, nature | *The Very Hungry Caterpillar*, *Planting a Rainbow*, *Last Day Hooray!* |

### Comprehension Strategies
- Picture walks before reading
- Think-alouds during reading
- Story retelling with puppets or drawings
- Story maps (beginning, middle, end)
- Turn and talk with a partner

### Standards Covered
- RL.K.1 – Ask and answer questions about key details
- RL.K.2 – Retell familiar stories, including key details
- RL.K.3 – Identify characters, settings, and major events
- RL.K.9 – Compare and contrast the adventures and experiences of characters
- RL.K.10 – Actively engage in group reading activities with purpose and understanding
- RI.K.1–RI.K.10 – Informational text standards (parallel structure)

## Strand 4: Writing (Weeks 1–36, ongoing)

### Learning Goals
- Use a combination of drawing, dictating, and writing to narrate, inform, and state an opinion
- Write upper- and lowercase letters
- Spell simple words phonetically
- Write first and last name

### Writing Units

| Unit | Weeks | Focus | Activities |
|------|-------|-------|------------|
| Drawing & labeling | 1–6 | Express ideas through pictures and labels | Draw and label, picture journals |
| Narrative writing | 7–14 | Tell a story with drawing and writing | Personal narratives, "small moments" |
| Opinion writing | 15–22 | State a preference with a reason | "My favorite ___", book reviews |
| Informational writing | 23–30 | Teach about a topic | "All about" books, how-to writing |
| Writing celebration | 31–36 | Publish and share | Author's chair, class book, portfolios |

### Standards Covered
- W.K.1 – Use a combination of drawing, dictating, and writing to compose opinion pieces
- W.K.2 – Use a combination of drawing, dictating, and writing to compose informative/explanatory texts
- W.K.3 – Use a combination of drawing, dictating, and writing to narrate a single event or several loosely linked events
- L.K.1 – Demonstrate command of the conventions of standard English grammar
- L.K.2 – Demonstrate command of the conventions of standard English capitalization, punctuation, and spelling

## Strand 5: Speaking & Listening (Weeks 1–36, ongoing)

### Learning Goals
- Participate in collaborative conversations
- Follow agreed-upon rules for discussions
- Ask and answer questions to seek help or clarify
- Describe familiar people, places, things, and events
- Speak audibly and express thoughts clearly

### Weekly Routines
- **Morning meeting**: Greeting, sharing, group activity, morning message
- **Show and tell**: Weekly presentations (1–2 minutes per student)
- **Turn and talk**: Partner discussions during read-alouds
- **Story retelling**: Oral retelling of stories read in class

### Standards Covered
- SL.K.1 – Participate in collaborative conversations with diverse partners
- SL.K.2 – Confirm understanding by asking questions and requesting clarification
- SL.K.3 – Ask and answer questions in order to seek help or get information
- SL.K.4 – Describe familiar people, places, things, and events
- SL.K.5 – Add drawings or other visual displays to descriptions
- SL.K.6 – Speak audibly and express thoughts, feelings, and ideas clearly

## Materials
- Decodable readers (levels A–D)
- Letter tiles, magnetic letters
- Sight word cards and word wall
- Writing journals, pencils, crayons
- Big books for shared reading
- Anchor charts for phonics and comprehension strategies
