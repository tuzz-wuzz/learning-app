# Kindergarten Mathematics Curriculum

Aligned with Common Core State Standards for Mathematics (CCSS-M).

---

## Strand 1: Counting and Cardinality (Weeks 1–12)

### Learning Goals
- Count to 100 by ones and by tens
- Count forward beginning from a given number within the known sequence
- Write numbers from 0 to 20. Represent a number of objects with a written numeral 0-20
- Understand the relationship between numbers and quantities; connect counting to cardinality
- Count to tell the number of objects (up to 20)
- Compare two numbers between 1 and 10 presented as written numerals

### Week-by-Week Breakdown

| Week | Topic | Key Activities |
|------|-------|----------------|
| 1 | Numbers 0–3 | Identification, tracing, counting small sets of objects |
| 2 | Numbers 4–5 | One-to-one correspondence, "How many?" games |
| 3 | Review 0–5 | Number line jumps, matching numerals to quantities |
| 4 | Numbers 6–8 | Counting physical manipulatives, writing numerals |
| 5 | Numbers 9–10 | Introduction to the 10-frame, "filling the frame" |
| 6 | Comparing Sets (0–10) | Using terms "greater than," "less than," and "equal to" |
| 7 | Counting to 50 by ones | Rote counting songs, daily calendar counting |
| 8 | Numbers 11–15 | Teen numbers introduction, 10-frames + extra ones |
| 9 | Numbers 16–20 | Building and writing teen numbers |
| 10 | Counting to 100 by tens | Choral counting, "Blast Off" games |
| 11 | Counting to 100 by ones | Hundred chart exploration, number patterns |
| 12 | Review & Assessment | Number recognition 0–20, counting assessment |

### Standards Covered
- K.CC.1 – Count to 100 by ones and by tens
- K.CC.2 – Count forward beginning from a given number
- K.CC.3 – Write numbers from 0 to 20
- K.CC.4 – Understand the relationship between numbers and quantities
- K.CC.5 – Count to tell the number of objects
- K.CC.6 – Identify whether the number of objects in one group is greater than, less than, or equal to the number of objects in another group
- K.CC.7 – Compare two numbers between 1 and 10

## Strand 2: Operations and Algebraic Thinking (Weeks 13–24)

### Learning Goals
- Represent addition and subtraction with objects, fingers, mental images, drawings, or sounds
- Solve addition and subtraction word problems within 10
- Decompose numbers less than or equal to 10 into pairs in more than one way
- For any number from 1 to 9, find the number that makes 10 when added to the given number
- Fluently add and subtract within 5

### Week-by-Week Breakdown

| Week | Topic | Key Activities |
|------|-------|----------------|
| 13 | Intro to Addition (within 5) | "Putting together" with blocks, addition symbols (+, =) |
| 14 | Addition Stories | Modeling word problems with drawings and counters |
| 15 | Intro to Subtraction (within 5) | "Taking away" games, subtraction symbols (-, =) |
| 16 | Subtraction Stories | Acting out "taking away" scenarios |
| 17 | Fluency within 5 | Speed games, flash cards, "Five Frame" activities |
| 18 | Addition within 10 | Part-Part-Whole mats, using two-color counters |
| 19 | Subtraction within 10 | "Bowling" subtraction, crossing out drawings |
| 20 | Number Bonds (to 10) | Decomposing 10 in different ways (e.g., 6+4, 7+3) |
| 21 | Making 10 | "Friends of 10" rainbow, 10-frame completion |
| 22 | Word Problems (Mixed) | Determining whether to add or subtract |
| 23 | Math Talk & Strategies | Explaining "how I solved it" (mental math vs counters) |
| 24 | Review & Assessment | Solving simple equations, fluency check within 5 |

### Standards Covered
- K.OA.1 – Represent addition and subtraction in multiple ways
- K.OA.2 – Solve addition and subtraction word problems within 10
- K.OA.3 – Decompose numbers less than or equal to 10
- K.OA.4 – For any number from 1 to 9, find the number that makes 10
- K.OA.5 – Fluently add and subtract within 5

## Strand 3: Number and Operations in Base Ten (Weeks 25–28)

### Learning Goals
- Work with numbers 11–19 to gain foundations for place value
- Compose and decompose numbers from 11 to 19 into ten ones and some further ones

### Week-by-Week Breakdown

| Week | Topic | Key Activities |
|------|-------|----------------|
| 25 | Foundations of Ten | Bundling 10 popsicle sticks, "The Power of Ten" |
| 26 | Numbers 11–15 (Base Ten) | Building numbers with 10-frames and extra counters |
| 27 | Numbers 16–19 (Base Ten) | Writing equations like 10 + 6 = 16 |
| 28 | Base Ten Review | Place value mats (Tens and Ones) |

### Standards Covered
- K.NBT.1 – Compose and decompose numbers from 11 to 19 into ten ones and some further ones

## Strand 4: Measurement and Data (Weeks 29–32)

### Learning Goals
- Describe measurable attributes of objects, such as length or weight
- Directly compare two objects with a measurable attribute in common
- Classify objects into given categories; count the numbers of objects in each category

### Week-by-Week Breakdown

| Week | Topic | Key Activities |
|------|-------|----------------|
| 29 | Comparing Length | Using "longer" and "shorter," measuring with Unifix cubes |
| 30 | Comparing Weight/Capacity | Balance scales, "heavier" and "lighter," water play |
| 31 | Sorting and Classifying | Sorting by color, shape, or size |
| 32 | Data and Graphing | Creating simple bar graphs of sorted objects |

### Standards Covered
- K.MD.1 – Describe measurable attributes of objects
- K.MD.2 – Directly compare two objects with a measurable attribute in common
- K.MD.3 – Classify objects into given categories

---

## Strand 5: Geometry (Weeks 33–36)

### Learning Goals
- Describe objects in the environment using names of shapes
- Describe the relative positions of these objects (above, below, beside, in front of, behind, next to)
- Correctly name shapes regardless of their orientations or overall size
- Identify shapes as two-dimensional (flat) or three-dimensional (solid)
- Analyze and compare two- and three-dimensional shapes
- Compose simple shapes to form larger shapes

### Week-by-Week Breakdown

| Week | Topic | Key Activities |
|------|-------|----------------|
| 33 | 2D Shapes (Flat) | Square, Circle, Triangle, Rectangle, Hexagon |
| 34 | Positional Words | "Simon Says" with shapes, obstacle courses |
| 35 | 3D Shapes (Solid) | Cube, Cone, Cylinder, Sphere; shape hunts |
| 36 | Composing Shapes | Pattern blocks, building 3D structures |

### Standards Covered
- K.G.1 – Describe objects in the environment using names of shapes and positional words
- K.G.2 – Correctly name shapes
- K.G.3 – Identify shapes as 2D or 3D
- K.G.4 – Analyze and compare 2D and 3D shapes
- K.G.5 – Model shapes in the world by building and drawing
- K.G.6 – Compose simple shapes to form larger shapes

## Materials
- Manipulatives (counters, Unifix cubes, bears)
- 10-frames and double 10-frames
- Balance scales
- Pattern blocks
- 3D geometric solids
- Number lines and hundred charts
- Dice and playing cards
- Writing journals and dry-erase boards
