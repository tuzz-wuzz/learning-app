# Kindergarten History & Social Studies Curriculum

Aligned with the C3 Framework for Social Studies and common state standards.

---

## Core Themes
- Self and community
- Rules and responsibilities
- American symbols and holidays
- Maps and geography basics
- Understanding the past

---

## Unit 1: All About Me (Weeks 1–4)

### Big Idea
Each person is unique and part of a family and community.

### Learning Goals
- Share personal information (name, age, family members, likes)
- Recognize similarities and differences among classmates
- Understand that each person has a personal history

### Week-by-Week Breakdown

| Week | Topic | Key Activities |
|------|-------|----------------|
| 1 | Who am I? | Self-portraits, "All About Me" books, name writing |
| 2 | My family | Family drawings, family tree, sharing about family traditions |
| 3 | Similarities & differences | Venn diagram comparisons, class surveys, graphing |
| 4 | My personal history | Timeline of my life, baby photo matching, growth discussion |

---

## Unit 2: Rules & Responsibilities (Weeks 5–8)

### Big Idea
Communities need rules to keep people safe and treat each other fairly.

### Learning Goals
- Understand why we have rules at home, school, and in the community
- Identify classroom rules and their purposes
- Practice being a responsible member of the classroom community
- Understand fairness and taking turns

### Week-by-Week Breakdown

| Week | Topic | Key Activities |
|------|-------|----------------|
| 5 | Classroom rules | Creating class rules together, rule posters |
| 6 | School rules | School tour, meeting school helpers, safety rules |
| 7 | Community rules | Traffic signs, playground rules, "what if" scenarios |
| 8 | Rights & responsibilities | Classroom jobs, taking care of shared spaces |

---

## Unit 3: My Community (Weeks 9–13)

### Big Idea
People in a community live, work, and help each other.

### Learning Goals
- Identify community helpers and their roles
- Understand the concept of needs vs. wants
- Recognize places in the community (school, library, fire station, hospital)
- Understand how people in a community depend on each other

### Week-by-Week Breakdown

| Week | Topic | Key Activities |
|------|-------|----------------|
| 9 | What is a community? | Neighborhood walk, community map, discussion |
| 10 | Community helpers: safety | Police, firefighters, EMTs — visitor or video, role play |
| 11 | Community helpers: services | Mail carrier, teacher, doctor, librarian — role play |
| 12 | Needs vs. wants | Sorting activity, discussion, picture sorting |
| 13 | Our school community | Thank-you cards, community helper day |

---

## Unit 4: Maps & Geography (Weeks 14–18)

### Big Idea
Maps help us understand where things are; the Earth has many different places.

### Learning Goals
- Understand basic map concepts (symbols, directions)
- Create simple maps (classroom, school, neighborhood)
- Identify land and water on a globe
- Learn cardinal directions (north, south, east, west)
- Locate the United States on a map/globe

### Week-by-Week Breakdown

| Week | Topic | Key Activities |
|------|-------|----------------|
| 14 | What is a map? | Bird's-eye view drawings, toy map activity |
| 15 | Classroom & school maps | Draw classroom map, map symbols, treasure hunt |
| 16 | Neighborhood maps | Walking map, places in our neighborhood |
| 17 | Globes & continents | Globe exploration, land vs. water, continent song |
| 18 | Where do I live? | Address practice, nesting location (room → school → city → state → country) |

---

## Unit 5: American Symbols & Holidays (Weeks 19–27)

### Big Idea
The United States has symbols, traditions, and holidays that represent our shared history and values.

### Learning Goals
- Recognize American symbols (flag, Statue of Liberty, bald eagle, White House)
- Understand the meaning of the Pledge of Allegiance
- Learn about national holidays and why we celebrate them
- Identify the current President

### Week-by-Week Breakdown

| Week | Topic | Key Activities |
|------|-------|----------------|
| 19 | The American flag | Flag craft, counting stars and stripes, Pledge of Allegiance |
| 20 | Statue of Liberty & bald eagle | Symbol matching, symbol coloring, read-alouds |
| 21 | The White House & the President | Picture study, "If I were President" writing |
| 22 | Martin Luther King Jr. Day | Read-aloud, kindness pledge, dream writing |
| 23 | Presidents' Day | Washington & Lincoln, coin study, president facts |
| 24 | Thanksgiving history | Pilgrims & Native Americans, thankfulness activities |
| 25 | Independence Day (4th of July) | American birthday, firework art, patriotic songs |
| 26 | Memorial Day & Veterans Day | Honoring service members, thank-you letters |
| 27 | Other holidays | Cultural holidays in our class, sharing traditions |

---

## Unit 6: Understanding the Past (Weeks 28–32)

### Big Idea
Things change over time; we can learn about the past from stories, objects, and pictures.

### Learning Goals
- Understand concepts of past, present, and future
- Compare life "long ago" to life today
- Sequence events in chronological order
- Understand that history is the story of real people and events

### Week-by-Week Breakdown

| Week | Topic | Key Activities |
|------|-------|----------------|
| 28 | Yesterday, today, tomorrow | Daily timeline, sequencing activities |
| 29 | Long ago vs. today: school | Compare old/new schoolhouses, tools, activities |
| 30 | Long ago vs. today: home | Compare old/new homes, toys, technology |
| 31 | Long ago vs. today: transportation | Horse & buggy to cars, trains, planes — sorting |
| 32 | Family history | Interview a family member, bring a family artifact |

---

## Unit 7: Being a Good Citizen (Weeks 33–36)

### Big Idea
Good citizens are responsible, respectful, and caring members of their community.

### Learning Goals
- Define citizenship and what it means to be a good citizen
- Practice kindness, honesty, respect, and responsibility
- Understand voting as a way to make fair decisions
- Participate in a class community service project

### Week-by-Week Breakdown

| Week | Topic | Key Activities |
|------|-------|----------------|
| 33 | What is a citizen? | Citizenship discussion, good citizen traits chart |
| 34 | Voting & decision-making | Class votes (book, snack, activity), voting process |
| 35 | Helping others | Community service project, kindness challenge |
| 36 | Year-end celebration | Citizenship awards, "What I learned" sharing, portfolio review |
