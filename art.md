# Kindergarten Art Curriculum

Aligned with National Core Arts Standards for Visual Arts.

---

## Program Goals
- Develop fine motor skills through various art media
- Explore elements of art: line, shape, color, texture, form, space
- Express ideas and feelings through artistic creation
- Build art vocabulary and appreciation
- Explore art from diverse cultures and time periods

---

## Unit 1: Lines & Shapes (Weeks 1–6)

### Learning Goals
- Identify and draw different types of lines (straight, curved, zigzag, wavy, dotted, spiral)
- Recognize and create basic shapes in artwork
- Understand that lines and shapes are building blocks of art

### Week-by-Week Breakdown

| Week | Topic | Key Activities |
|------|-------|----------------|
| 1 | Exploring lines | Line walks, line drawings, line collage |
| 2 | Lines in art | Study Mondrian's lines, create line art |
| 3 | Basic shapes | Shape collage, shape stamping, shape hunt |
| 4 | Shapes in art | Study Kandinsky's circles, geometric art |
| 5 | Self-portraits | Using shapes and lines to draw faces, mirror observation |
| 6 | Shape animals | Create animals using basic shapes, display |

### Artist Spotlight
- Piet Mondrian (lines and rectangles)
- Wassily Kandinsky (circles and shapes)

---

## Unit 2: Color (Weeks 7–12)

### Learning Goals
- Identify and name primary colors (red, yellow, blue)
- Discover secondary colors through color mixing
- Explore warm and cool colors
- Use color to express mood and feeling

### Week-by-Week Breakdown

| Week | Topic | Key Activities |
|------|-------|----------------|
| 7 | Primary colors | Finger painting with primaries, color sorting |
| 8 | Color mixing | Mixing primaries to make secondaries, color wheels |
| 9 | Warm colors | Sunset paintings, warm color collage |
| 10 | Cool colors | Ocean/sky paintings, cool color collage |
| 11 | Colors & feelings | "How does this color make you feel?" discussion and painting |
| 12 | Rainbow art | Rainbow order, tissue paper rainbows |

### Artist Spotlight
- Henri Matisse (bold colors, cut-outs)
- Mark Rothko (color fields and emotion)

---

## Unit 3: Texture (Weeks 13–16)

### Learning Goals
- Understand texture as how something feels or looks like it feels
- Create visual and tactile textures in artwork
- Use different materials to explore texture

### Week-by-Week Breakdown

| Week | Topic | Key Activities |
|------|-------|----------------|
| 13 | What is texture? | Texture walk, texture rubbings, touch-and-feel boards |
| 14 | Collage textures | Collage with varied materials (fabric, sandpaper, foil, yarn) |
| 15 | Nature textures | Leaf prints, bark rubbings, nature collage |
| 16 | Texture in sculpture | Playdough texturing, clay imprints |

### Artist Spotlight
- Eric Carle (textured illustrations)

---

## Unit 4: Painting Techniques (Weeks 17–21)

### Learning Goals
- Hold and use a paintbrush correctly
- Explore different painting tools and techniques
- Practice brush care and cleanup routines
- Create expressive paintings

### Week-by-Week Breakdown

| Week | Topic | Key Activities |
|------|-------|----------------|
| 17 | Brush painting basics | Brush strokes, thick and thin lines, brush care |
| 18 | Sponge & stamp painting | Sponge prints, vegetable stamps, found-object printing |
| 19 | Watercolor painting | Watercolor techniques (wet-on-wet, wet-on-dry), landscapes |
| 20 | Finger painting | Free expression, pattern making, color mixing |
| 21 | Splatter & drip painting | Study Pollock, outdoor splatter painting, action art |

### Artist Spotlight
- Jackson Pollock (action painting)
- Claude Monet (impressionist landscapes)

---

## Unit 5: Sculpture & 3D Art (Weeks 22–26)

### Learning Goals
- Understand the difference between 2D and 3D art
- Use various materials to create sculptures
- Explore form and space
- Build and construct using recycled materials

### Week-by-Week Breakdown

| Week | Topic | Key Activities |
|------|-------|----------------|
| 22 | What is sculpture? | Gallery of 3D art images, handling clay |
| 23 | Playdough & clay | Pinch pots, coil pots, clay animals |
| 24 | Paper sculpture | Paper folding, curling, fringing, standing sculptures |
| 25 | Recycled art | Building sculptures from boxes, tubes, bottle caps |
| 26 | Group sculpture | Collaborative class sculpture project |

### Artist Spotlight
- Alexander Calder (mobiles and stabiles)
- Louise Nevelson (assembled sculptures)

---

## Unit 6: Printmaking & Pattern (Weeks 27–30)

### Learning Goals
- Create simple prints using stamps and found objects
- Understand and create repeating patterns (AB, ABB, ABC)
- Explore symmetry in art and nature

### Week-by-Week Breakdown

| Week | Topic | Key Activities |
|------|-------|----------------|
| 27 | Stamping & printing | Foam stamps, veggie prints, handprint art |
| 28 | Patterns in art | Pattern strips, patterned borders, quilt squares |
| 29 | Symmetry | Butterfly symmetry painting, fold-and-print |
| 30 | Pattern & print project | Design wrapping paper or fabric using prints and patterns |

### Artist Spotlight
- Andy Warhol (pop art prints)
- Alma Thomas (patterned paintings)

---

## Unit 7: Art Around the World (Weeks 31–34)

### Learning Goals
- Explore art traditions from diverse cultures
- Understand that art is made by people everywhere
- Create art inspired by world traditions

### Week-by-Week Breakdown

| Week | Topic | Key Activities |
|------|-------|----------------|
| 31 | African art | Kente cloth patterns, mask making |
| 32 | Japanese art | Cherry blossom painting, origami |
| 33 | Mexican art | Papel picado (paper cutting), sun faces |
| 34 | Aboriginal Australian art | Dot painting, dreamtime stories |

### Artist Spotlight
- Frida Kahlo (self-portraits, Mexican art)
- Katsushika Hokusai (Japanese woodblock prints)

---

## Unit 8: Art Show & Celebration (Weeks 35–36)

### Learning Goals
- Reflect on the year's learning
- Select and present favorite works
- Practice talking about art using art vocabulary
- Celebrate creative growth

### Activities
- Portfolio review and selection
- Artist statements ("I made this because...")
- Classroom or school art show
- Gallery walk with families

---

## Elements of Art Reference

| Element | Definition | Kindergarten Focus |
|---------|-----------|-------------------|
| Line | A mark made by a moving point | Straight, curved, zigzag, wavy |
| Shape | A closed line (2D) | Circle, square, triangle, rectangle, oval |
| Color | What we see when light reflects off an object | Primary, secondary, warm, cool |
| Texture | How something feels or looks like it feels | Rough, smooth, bumpy, soft |
| Form | A 3D shape | Sphere, cube, cylinder, cone |
| Space | The area around, between, or within objects | Foreground, background |

## Materials
- Crayons, markers, colored pencils
- Tempera paint, watercolors, finger paint
- Paintbrushes (various sizes), sponges
- Construction paper, tissue paper, cardstock
- Glue, scissors, tape
- Playdough, air-dry clay
- Collage materials (fabric scraps, yarn, buttons, beads)
- Recycled materials for sculpture
- Printing supplies (foam sheets, ink pads)
