# Kindergarten Music Curriculum

Aligned with National Core Arts Standards for Music.

---

## Program Goals
- Develop a love of music through singing, playing, moving, and listening
- Build foundational music skills: steady beat, rhythm, pitch, dynamics
- Explore music from diverse cultures and traditions
- Connect music to other subject areas
- Encourage creative expression through music-making

---

## Unit 1: Exploring Sound (Weeks 1–4)

### Learning Goals
- Distinguish between music and noise
- Identify sounds in the environment
- Explore how sounds are made (vibrations)
- Understand loud/soft (dynamics) and fast/slow (tempo)

### Week-by-Week Breakdown

| Week | Topic | Key Activities |
|------|-------|----------------|
| 1 | Sounds around us | Sound walk, sound bingo, listening journal |
| 2 | How sound is made | Vibration experiments, rubber band guitar, drum exploration |
| 3 | Loud & soft (dynamics) | Whispering vs. shouting songs, dynamic contrasts |
| 4 | Fast & slow (tempo) | Moving to fast/slow music, tempo games, animal tempos |

### Vocabulary
- Sound, music, noise, loud (forte), soft (piano), fast, slow, vibration

---

## Unit 2: Steady Beat & Rhythm (Weeks 5–10)

### Learning Goals
- Feel and maintain a steady beat
- Distinguish between beat and rhythm
- Clap, tap, and move to the beat
- Perform simple rhythmic patterns

### Week-by-Week Breakdown

| Week | Topic | Key Activities |
|------|-------|----------------|
| 5 | Finding the beat | Heartbeat listening, clapping to songs, marching |
| 6 | Beat in songs | Patting the beat, beat instruments, movement |
| 7 | Beat vs. rhythm | Clapping words vs. beat, name rhythms |
| 8 | Long & short sounds | Ta and ti-ti introduction, rhythm sticks |
| 9 | Rhythm patterns | Echo clapping, rhythm cards, body percussion |
| 10 | Beat & rhythm review | Rhythm games, musical chairs, performance |

### Songs & Activities
- "We Will Rock You" (beat)
- "Baa Baa Black Sheep" (rhythm vs. beat)
- Name rhythm clapping
- Echo clapping games

### Vocabulary
- Beat, rhythm, pattern, long, short, ta (quarter note), ti-ti (eighth notes)

---

## Unit 3: Singing & Voice (Weeks 11–16)

### Learning Goals
- Use singing voice vs. speaking voice vs. whispering voice vs. calling voice
- Match pitch and sing in tune
- Sing a variety of songs from memory
- Explore high and low sounds (pitch)

### Week-by-Week Breakdown

| Week | Topic | Key Activities |
|------|-------|----------------|
| 11 | Types of voices | Voice exploration, singing vs. speaking games |
| 12 | High & low pitch | Pitch matching, hand signals (Curwen), slide whistle |
| 13 | Sol-mi songs | "Rain Rain Go Away", "See-Saw", hand signs |
| 14 | Sol-mi-la songs | "Ring Around the Rosie", "Hot Cross Buns" |
| 15 | Song collection | Learning seasonal and thematic songs, song favorites |
| 16 | Singing review | Group singing performance, singing games |

### Core Song Repertoire
- "Twinkle Twinkle Little Star"
- "The Itsy Bitsy Spider"
- "If You're Happy and You Know It"
- "Old MacDonald Had a Farm"
- "Head, Shoulders, Knees, and Toes"
- "The Wheels on the Bus"
- "Rain, Rain, Go Away"
- "Row, Row, Row Your Boat"

### Vocabulary
- Singing voice, speaking voice, high, low, pitch, melody, sol, mi, la

---

## Unit 4: Instruments (Weeks 17–22)

### Learning Goals
- Identify and play classroom percussion instruments
- Understand instrument families (shake, scrape, strike)
- Play instruments with proper technique
- Accompany songs on instruments

### Week-by-Week Breakdown

| Week | Topic | Key Activities |
|------|-------|----------------|
| 17 | Shaking instruments | Maracas, shakers, egg shakers — technique and exploration |
| 18 | Striking instruments | Drums, wood blocks, triangles — proper grip and striking |
| 19 | Scraping instruments | Guiro, sand blocks, rhythm sticks — technique |
| 20 | Instrument sorting | Sort by family, instrument bingo, listening identification |
| 21 | Playing together | Simple arrangements, following a conductor, group performance |
| 22 | Instrument exploration | Free exploration stations, composing simple pieces |

### Instruments
- Rhythm sticks, wood blocks, sand blocks
- Triangles, finger cymbals, hand drums
- Maracas, shakers, tambourines
- Guiro, jingle bells, tone bells
- Xylophones (if available)

### Vocabulary
- Percussion, shake, scrape, strike, conductor, instrument names

---

## Unit 5: Movement & Dance (Weeks 23–28)

### Learning Goals
- Move expressively to music
- Demonstrate locomotor movements (walk, run, skip, gallop, hop, jump)
- Respond to musical elements through movement (tempo, dynamics, pitch)
- Learn simple folk dances and singing games

### Week-by-Week Breakdown

| Week | Topic | Key Activities |
|------|-------|----------------|
| 23 | Walking & marching | March to Sousa, walking to the beat, freeze dance |
| 24 | Running, skipping, galloping | Movement to different tempos, animal movements |
| 25 | Creative movement | Moving like the music (ocean, wind, animals, machines) |
| 26 | Freeze dance | Freeze when music stops, statue poses, body shapes |
| 27 | Folk dances | "Hokey Pokey", "Looby Loo", "Shoo Fly" |
| 28 | Dancing review | Dance party, student-chosen favorites, performance |

### Vocabulary
- Locomotor, non-locomotor, freeze, tempo, movement, choreography

---

## Unit 6: Listening & Appreciation (Weeks 29–33)

### Learning Goals
- Listen attentively to a variety of music
- Identify mood and feeling in music
- Recognize some orchestral instruments by sight and sound
- Explore music from different cultures and time periods

### Week-by-Week Breakdown

| Week | Topic | Key Activities |
|------|-------|----------------|
| 29 | Listening skills | Active listening exercises, drawing what you hear |
| 30 | Mood in music | Happy/sad/scary/exciting music, drawing responses |
| 31 | Orchestral instruments | String, wind, brass, percussion — video & audio clips |
| 32 | Music around the world | African drumming, Indian ragas, Chinese music, Latin rhythms |
| 33 | Music stories | "Peter and the Wolf", "Carnival of the Animals" |

### Listening Repertoire
- Prokofiev: *Peter and the Wolf*
- Saint-Saëns: *Carnival of the Animals*
- Grieg: *In the Hall of the Mountain King*
- Sousa: *Stars and Stripes Forever*
- Beethoven: *Ode to Joy*
- Traditional music from Africa, Asia, Latin America

### Vocabulary
- Orchestra, composer, strings, winds, brass, percussion, mood

---

## Unit 7: Creating & Performing (Weeks 34–36)

### Learning Goals
- Create simple musical compositions
- Perform for an audience
- Reflect on musical learning throughout the year

### Week-by-Week Breakdown

| Week | Topic | Key Activities |
|------|-------|----------------|
| 34 | Composing | Create simple rhythmic patterns, sound stories |
| 35 | Rehearsal | Practice songs and pieces for performance |
| 36 | Performance & celebration | Class music concert, sharing with families, year reflection |

## Cross-Curricular Connections

| Subject | Music Connection |
|---------|-----------------|
| Math | Counting songs, patterns, beat counting |
| English | Song lyrics, rhyming, storytelling through music |
| Spanish | Spanish songs and rhymes |
| Science | Sound and vibrations, animal songs |
| History | Patriotic songs, cultural music traditions |
| Art | Drawing responses to music, instrument crafts |

## Daily/Weekly Routines
- **Hello song**: Begin each class with a greeting song
- **Goodbye song**: End each class with a closing song
- **Transition songs**: Use songs for cleanup, lining up, and transitions
- **Calendar songs**: Days of the week, months of the year songs

- Scarves, ribbons, and streamers for movement
- Rhythm cards and flashcards
- Picture books with musical themes
- Recordings of diverse musical genres and cultures
