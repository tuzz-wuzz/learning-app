# Kindergarten Science Curriculum

Aligned with Next Generation Science Standards (NGSS) for Kindergarten.

---

## Core Practices (Integrated Throughout)
- Asking questions and defining problems
- Planning and carrying out investigations
- Analyzing and interpreting data
- Constructing explanations
- Engaging in argument from evidence
- Obtaining, evaluating, and communicating information

---

## Unit 1: My Five Senses (Weeks 1–4)

### Big Idea
We use our senses to observe and learn about the world around us.

### Learning Goals
- Name the five senses and their associated body parts
- Use senses to observe and describe objects and materials
- Sort objects based on observable properties

### Week-by-Week Breakdown

| Week | Topic | Key Activities |
|------|-------|----------------|
| 1 | Sight & hearing | Mystery bag observations, sound walk, blindfold games |
| 2 | Touch & smell | Texture sorting, scent jars, descriptive vocabulary |
| 3 | Taste & review | Taste-testing stations (sweet, salty, sour, bitter), safety rules |
| 4 | Using all senses together | Observation journals, nature walk, science notebooks |

---

## Unit 2: Weather & Seasons (Weeks 5–9)

### Big Idea
Weather changes from day to day and season to season; weather patterns can be observed and described.

### Learning Goals
- Observe, describe, and record daily weather conditions
- Identify types of weather (sunny, cloudy, rainy, snowy, windy)
- Recognize the four seasons and their characteristics
- Understand how weather affects what we wear and do

### Week-by-Week Breakdown

| Week | Topic | Key Activities |
|------|-------|----------------|
| 5 | What is weather? | Weather vocabulary, daily weather chart |
| 6 | Sunny & cloudy days | Sun observations (safe!), cloud types, cotton ball clouds |
| 7 | Rain & snow | Rain gauge, water cycle introduction, snowflake crafts |
| 8 | Wind | Windsock making, pinwheel craft, wind observation |
| 9 | Seasons | Season sorting, season trees, weather data graphing |

### NGSS Standards
- K-ESS2-1 – Use and share observations of local weather conditions to describe patterns over time
- K-ESS3-2 – Ask questions to obtain information about the purpose of weather forecasting to prepare for and respond to severe weather

---

## Unit 3: Plants (Weeks 10–14)

### Big Idea
Plants are living things that need specific things to grow and survive.

### Learning Goals
- Identify parts of a plant (roots, stem, leaves, flower, seed)
- Understand that plants need water, sunlight, and air to grow
- Observe and record plant growth over time
- Understand the plant life cycle (seed → sprout → plant → flower → seed)

### Week-by-Week Breakdown

| Week | Topic | Key Activities |
|------|-------|----------------|
| 10 | Parts of a plant | Plant dissection, labeling diagrams, garden walk |
| 11 | What plants need | Plant experiment: light vs. dark, water vs. no water |
| 12 | Seeds & germination | Seed planting, seed sorting, baggie greenhouse |
| 13 | Plant life cycle | Observing growth, measuring, journaling |
| 14 | Plants we eat | Tasting plant parts (roots=carrots, leaves=lettuce, etc.) |

### NGSS Standards
- K-LS1-1 – Use observations to describe patterns of what plants and animals need to survive
- K-ESS3-1 – Use a model to represent the relationship between the needs of different plants and the places they live

---

## Unit 4: Animals (Weeks 15–20)

### Big Idea
Animals are living things with basic needs; different animals live in different habitats.

### Learning Goals
- Identify basic needs of animals (food, water, shelter, air)
- Describe similarities and differences between animals
- Understand animal habitats (forest, ocean, desert, farm)
- Explore animal life cycles (egg, baby, adult)
- Classify animals (mammals, birds, fish, reptiles, insects)

### Week-by-Week Breakdown

| Week | Topic | Key Activities |
|------|-------|----------------|
| 15 | Animal needs | Compare to human needs, animal care discussion |
| 16 | Animal groups | Sorting animals by type, animal features chart |
| 17 | Habitats: forest & ocean | Habitat dioramas, animal matching |
| 18 | Habitats: desert & farm | Habitat sorting, animal research (choose an animal) |
| 19 | Animal babies | Matching parents to babies, life cycle of a butterfly |
| 20 | Animal review | Animal fair, presentations, animal books |

### NGSS Standards
- K-LS1-1 – Use observations to describe patterns of what plants and animals need to survive
- K-ESS2-2 – Construct an argument supported by evidence for how plants and animals can change the environment to meet their needs

---

## Unit 5: Pushes & Pulls (Forces and Motion) (Weeks 21–26)

### Big Idea
Pushes and pulls can change the speed or direction of an object's motion.

### Learning Goals
- Identify pushes and pulls in everyday life
- Investigate how the strength of a push or pull changes an object's motion
- Explore how the size and shape of an object affect its motion
- Design a solution to change an object's motion

### Week-by-Week Breakdown

| Week | Topic | Key Activities |
|------|-------|----------------|
| 21 | What are pushes & pulls? | Push/pull sort, playground observations |
| 22 | Strength of force | Ramp experiments, car rolling, distance measuring |
| 23 | Direction of motion | Ball rolling, bowling, directional vocabulary |
| 24 | Collisions | Marble runs, domino chains, crash investigations |
| 25 | Gravity | Drop tests, parachute making, feather vs. ball |
| 26 | Engineering challenge | Design a way to move a ball into a cup |

### NGSS Standards
- K-PS2-1 – Plan and conduct an investigation to compare the effects of different strengths or different directions of pushes and pulls on the motion of an object
- K-PS2-2 – Analyze data to determine if a design solution works as intended to change the speed or direction of an object with a push or a pull

---

## Unit 6: Materials & Their Properties (Weeks 27–31)

### Big Idea
Objects can be described, compared, and sorted by the properties of the materials from which they are made.

### Learning Goals
- Describe objects by color, shape, size, weight, texture, flexibility
- Compare and sort materials by their properties
- Investigate which materials are best for specific purposes
- Explore what happens when materials are heated or cooled

### Week-by-Week Breakdown

| Week | Topic | Key Activities |
|------|-------|----------------|
| 27 | Describing materials | Material exploration stations, descriptive vocabulary |
| 28 | Sorting by properties | Sorting mats, Venn diagrams, attribute games |
| 29 | Sink or float | Prediction and testing, recording results |
| 30 | Building & testing | Three Little Pigs engineering challenge |
| 31 | Heating & cooling | Ice melting investigations, warming observations |

### NGSS Standards
- K-PS3-1 – Make observations to determine the effect of sunlight on Earth's surface
- K-2-ETS1-1 – Ask questions, make observations, and gather information about a situation people want to change to define a simple problem that can be solved through the development of a new or improved object or tool

---

## Unit 7: Earth & Sky (Weeks 32–36)

### Big Idea
Earth is made of different materials; the sun, moon, and stars can be observed in the sky.

### Learning Goals
- Identify natural features of the Earth (land, water, rocks, soil)
- Observe patterns of the sun and moon
- Explore day and night
- Understand how to reduce human impact on the environment

### Week-by-Week Breakdown

| Week | Topic | Key Activities |
|------|-------|----------------|
| 32 | Land & water | Globe exploration, land/water sorting, sand & water table |
| 33 | Rocks & soil | Rock collection, soil observation, magnifying glasses |
| 34 | Sun & shadows | Shadow tracing, sundial making, sun safety |
| 35 | Moon & stars | Moon phase observation, constellation crafts |
| 36 | Taking care of Earth | Reduce/reuse/recycle, Earth Day activities, nature pledges |

### NGSS Standards
- K-ESS2-1 – Use and share observations of local weather conditions to describe patterns over time
- K-ESS3-3 – Communicate solutions that will reduce the impact of humans on the land, water, air, and/or other living things in the local environment

---

## Science Notebooks
Students maintain science notebooks throughout the year for:
- Drawing observations
- Recording predictions and results
- Labeling diagrams
- Pasting data collection sheets

---

## Materials
- Magnifying glasses, balance scales, measuring cups
- Seeds, soil, planting containers
- Ramps, balls, toy cars
- Assorted materials for sorting (fabric, wood, metal, plastic)
- Science notebooks and colored pencils
- Nature collection bins
